What is Food Production?



Food production is the process of growing, harvesting, processing, and distributing food to consumers. Food production is considered as the process of transforming raw materials into edible food products. This can be done directly at home or commercially at industries. The process of food production involves various stages and each stage is important. These stages start with the basic steps like cleaning, processing, segregation and ends with packing and marketing. In this article we are going to discuss food production in depth.

3. Methods of Food Production



In the food industry various methods are used for the production of food. Some of these methods can be used at home but some are complicated and can be used only in the food processing industries. There are several forms of food production that are used today. Food production and food processing are interrelated and hence the steps show overlapping. Food processing refers to the processes that change the raw materials into products which can either be consumed directly or need to undergo some process before eating. 4. Food System



The food system refers to the food chain which begins with raw materials and ends with the consumption of final food products. It includes the following stages:



A. Food production


Food production refers to processes of making ready to eat food at home or in industries. It includes agriculture, farming etc.


B. Distribution and aggregation


It involves the distribution of various types of food items


C. Food processing


It refers to the processes that change the raw materials into products which can either be consumed directly or need to undergo some process before eating.


D. Marketing


It involves the selling of the processed food items. This needs proper advertisements.


E. Purchasing


It involves the buying of processed food items by the customers from shops, restaurants, hotels etc.


F. Consumption


It is the utilisation of the food by the consumer.


G. Waste recovery


It involves the management of the wastes from the food production, processing, marketing and consumption levels. This step ensures that the output is maximum with minimum input and wastage. This can be done by proper food production management.


H. Food production management


It is the process of optimising and managing the food production. It involves the development and implementation of various plans to manage and control different levels of the food system. It ensures that all the parts involved are working in a coordinated manner and include the following steps:



  1. Purchasing of raw ingredients and materials.
  2. Hiring the required professionals. Examples include chefs, waiters etc.
  3. Creating proper budgets.
  4. Ensuring the quality of food served.
  5. Maintaining the infrastructure.
  6. Following protocols.
  7. Following cleanliness.
  8. Reducing wastage generation.
  9. Proper disposal of wastes.
  10. Maintaining the operational records.
  11. Proper handling of customers.
  12. Managing the inventory (the process of ordering, using, storing, and selling the inventory)



5. Advantages of Food Production

The following are somes of the advantages of food production:

  1. Food production is a vast industry which gives lots of opportunities.
  2. It gives employment to a large group of people like chefs, local vendors, waiters and farmers.
  3. Industries include restaurants, local food markets, grocery stores, online food delivery apps, agriculture etc.
  4. It is a source of income for the population, especially for home makers.
  5. It increases exports and helps in maintaining foreign relations.
  6. It helps in proper distribution of food.
  7. It makes the food available to all populations.
  8. The food production industry plays a significant role in the economy. A huge portion of the economy in countries like India and China comes from agriculture.




5. Disadvantages of Food Production

The following are somes of the disadvantages of food production:

  1. It causes climate changes. For example, stubble burning causes pollution in Delhi.
  2. It causes water and soil pollution especially by the use of chemical fertilisers, preservatives, weedicides etc.
  3. It causes the extinction of many species.
  4. It also causes greenhouse gas emissions and global warming.
